Want to chow down in L.A.?

Analysis of Yelp restaurant ratings.

The Data

Ratings, reviews and geographical data were accumulated courtesy of the Yelp developer api.

The Python scripts for the api pull and map visualized below live on this Github repository: LA_Restaurants_Yelp_API

Data cleanup and visualization utilizing Python Pandas mostly in Jupyter notebooks are here: stars_of_la GitHub repository

The data visualized below is explored in depth in the notebooks available in the github, and the csv files are available for download. Please feel free to clone the repository and I would love to hear about any insights you find in your own analysis. Link to my personal page is below.

20,000 restaurants analyzed

Data collection was restricted to Los Angeles zipcodes that start with "900" since that seemed like a reasonable way to keep close to city center and due to the way the api works, the resultant collection reflects a random sampling of almost 20,000 establishments from over 150 zipcodes.

Ratings Void

This histogram reflects total counts of ratings and reveals a gap in the 2.5 star category. For whatever reason reviewers were inclined to stick with 3 and above on the higher end and only up to 2 on the lower.

Skew towards 4

It seems like establishments that stay in business long enough are likely to wind up with a rating of about 4. The next series of visualizations show the correlation between review counts and ratings.

Breaking the mold

The outliers are revealing in this analysis. Although there seems to be a virtual tidal pull towards a mean rating of roughly 3.8 - 4.1 if an establishment could stay around long enough, there are a few that managed to defy the odds and either stay in business despite horrible reviews, or maintain top notch ratings along with high review counts.

Bold and bad

One zipcode 90045 stood out from the rest as a hotbed of rotten ratings. It is visually represented in the blue bubble chart above. The light blue circle towards the higher end of the review counts but showing an average rating of approximately 3.

The worst of the worst

This zipcode is also home to what might be the worst restaurant in all of LA, with a whoppingly poor rating average of 1.5, all while continuing to stay in business as reflected by relatively high number of reviews.

Captured audience?

Digging a little deeper into the zipcode revealed that this is dominated by restaurants catering to the Los Angeles International Airport. Perhaps they are able to sustain income inspite of poor ratings due to the sheer volume of people with little other option than to grab a bite close to accomodations or flight times.

Heads above the rest

On the positive end of the spectrum are a few places that seem worthy of making a special trip to patronize.

Most reviewed

Note that in the light pink and brown scatter plot there is an outlier in the average rating of 4 category that has over 17,000 reviews, nearly triple the median of review counts. This turns out to be: "Botega Louie"

The limitations, the plan

As is often the case, diving into analysis produces more questions and potentially interesting pursuits than actual conclusions. It is hard to draw conclusions as to the reasons for ratings. It is somewhat subjective by nature, and in the age of paid social influencers, sometimes even suspect. That being said, there are additonal data points that can be brought in from the developer api such as price category and the open data program supported by the city of Los Angeles allows substantial demographic content to be incorporated. The next step will be to incorporate a few more data points and run the data through some machine learning algorithms to see what more can be revealed.
